## **Act I: The Core Thesis**
### *Prompting as Cognitive Architecture*

The central thesis of The Induction Method is a fundamental paradigm shift: from "Prompting as Instruction" to **"Prompting as Cognitive Architecture."** We treat the LLM as a cognitive engine. The prompt becomes a structured environment of **Cognitive Pressure** designed to shape the model's internal reasoning process.

> **The Guiding Question of the Architect**
>
> Instead of asking, "How can I be more specific?" we ask: **"What mode of thinking does this task require, and how do I build a prompt that forces the model into that state?"**

## **Act II: The Doctrine**
### *The Levers of Cognitive Control*

Cognitive Pressure is an engineering discipline applied through several key levers.

> **The Master Lever: Persona Instantiation**
>
> The most powerful lever is the **Persona**. Instantiating a specific, expert persona is the single most effective way to shape the AI's entire probability space. It is the chassis upon which all other levers are mounted.

**The Four Supporting Levers:**

1.  **Process Externalization:** Forces step-by-step reasoning for logical tasks (e.g., Chain-of-Thought ).
2.  **Divergent & Convergent Thinking:** Forces exploration of multiple solutions before selection for strategic tasks (e.g., Tree of Thoughts).
3.  **Meta-Cognition:** Forces a switch from "generator" to "critic" for refinement and fact-checking (e.g., Self-Critique Loops).
4.  **Conceptual Blending:** Forces the synthesis of multiple concepts to generate novelty (e.g., "Expert Fusion" Personas).
